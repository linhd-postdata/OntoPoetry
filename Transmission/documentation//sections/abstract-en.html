Abstract

This ontology module enables the description of the different sources or manifestations of the textual editions of poetic works. It provides classes and properties to represent the ideal characteristics of publication products (bibliographic sources) as well as other unique physical editions of a poetic work such manuscripts (primary sources). The ontology can be used to decribe the physical characteristics (material, condition, style, etc.) of sources, but also parts of sources corresponding to specific locations or sections. The physical objects described include specific bibliographic products (items) and reproductions (facsimiles). These, in addition to primary sources, may have certain rights, curators, and owners assigned.
